About the Election System

The Election System of India is administered by an independent constitutional authority responsible for conducting free and fair elections across the country. It manages national, state, and local elections while ensuring transparency, neutrality, and integrity in the democratic process.

The commission supervises voter registration, electoral roll preparation, candidate nomination, polling operations, and declaration of results. It ensures that every eligible citizen can exercise their right to vote without discrimination.

Leadership & Key Officials

R. Kumar
Chief Election Commissioner

Responsible for supervising the entire electoral process of the country. Oversees the conduct of Parliamentary, State Assembly, and by-elections. Ensures that elections are free, fair, and transparent according to the Constitution and electoral laws. Approves election schedules, political party recognition, model code of conduct enforcement, and final result declaration.

S. Mehta
Election Commissioner

Manages voter registration and maintenance of the electoral roll database. Verifies voter eligibility, updates new voter applications, and removes duplicate or invalid entries. Supervises distribution of voter ID cards and ensures polling stations are properly assigned to voters.

A. Reddy
Election Commissioner

Supervises field-level election operations and polling station management. Coordinates with district election officers, polling personnel, and observers during elections. Ensures proper functioning of EVMs and VVPAT machines, monitors polling day procedures, and verifies security arrangements at booths.

P. Sharma
IT Director

Responsible for managing the online election services and digital infrastructure. Maintains voter portals, complaint systems, and online registration platforms. Ensures cybersecurity of voter data, database protection, and secure communication between election offices. Monitors website uptime, result publication systems, and technical support services during elections.

History of Voting in India

India conducted its first general election in 1951-52 after independence. Initially, paper ballot boxes were used for voting. With technological advancement, Electronic Voting Machines (EVMs) were introduced to improve accuracy and speed.

Today, elections are conducted with modern systems ensuring transparency and voter confidence in the democratic process.
